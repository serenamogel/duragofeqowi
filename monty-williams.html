Monty Williams is an American basketball coach, executive, and former player. Monty Williams is well-known as the head coach of the NBA team the Phoenix Suns who also won the NBA Coach of the Year award in 2022.

Early Life

Monty Williams was born on 8 October 1971 and he is currently 50 years old. Williams hails from Fredericksburg, Virginia, and his zodiac sign is Libra. Talking about his family, his parents are Joyce Williams and Tavares Williams.

Moreover, his full name is Monty Eli Williams and he follows Christianity. Regarding his educational journey, he attended the University of Notre Dame and played small forward on the university’s basketball team.

Career and Professional Life

Professionally, Monty Williams is a basketball coach and executive. In the past, Williams was also a basketball player. At present, this personality is the head coach for the National Basketball Association (NBA) team Phoenix Suns.

As a player, this personality played basketball for five NBA teams during a playing career spanning from 1994 to 2003.

Williams’ NBA coaching career has included stints as an assistant coach, an associate head coach, and a head coach. Moreover, this personality was the head coach for the New Orleans Hornets/Pelicans from 2010 until 2015.

Additionally, Williams served as an assistant coach with the United States national team under Mike Krzyzewski. Likewise, he also worked as a vice president of basketball operations for the San Antonio Spurs. In May 2019, the Phoenix Suns hired him as the head coach of the team.

More Details

More recently in 2021, this personality led the Suns to their first NBA Finals appearance since 1993. Furthermore, Williams was named the NBA Coach of the Year recently in 2022. This happened when the Suns finished the regular season with a franchise record of 64 wins.

Talking about the beginning of his coaching career, Williams won an NBA championship in the spring of 2005 as a coaching staff intern with the San Antonio Spurs.

Then in the fall of the same year, new head coach Nate McMillan hired this personality as an assistant coach for the Portland Trail Blazers.

Then on 29 June 2015, this personality became the associate head coach of the Oklahoma City Thunder. Likewise, on 1 June 2016, it was announced that this personality would not return with the Thunder.

Further, on 4 June 2018, Brett Brown announced that this personality would join his staff in Philadelphia as the lead assistant coach. This was Williams’ first coaching job in two seasons.
